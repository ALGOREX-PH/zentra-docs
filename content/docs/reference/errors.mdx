---
title: Errors
description: The contract Error enum (codes 1–8), with the symptom and likely cause of each.
---

`authorize_action` and the other contract methods return a typed `Error`. Each
code maps to a specific check in the [state machine](/docs/reference/contract).

| Code | Variant | What it means | Likely cause |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | `MalformedVerifyingKey` | The embedded verifying key didn't decode | Build/transcode mismatch in `vk.rs` |
| 2 | `PolicyNotFound` | No policy registered for (agent, commitment) | Policy never committed, or wrong commitment |
| 3 | `PolicyRevoked` | The policy was revoked | `revoke_policy` was called for it |
| 4 | `InvalidProof` | The Groth16 pairing check failed | Tampered proof/public inputs, or wrong circuit artifacts |
| 5 | `StateMismatch` | Proof's `prev_*` ≠ effective on-chain state | Stale state read, epoch drift, or a forged prior spend (Panel C) |
| 6 | `NullifierUsed` | The nullifier was already recorded | Replay, or a reused `nonce` |
| 7 | `InvalidAmount` | `amount` was not greater than zero | A zero or negative amount |
| 8 | `Overflow` | A counter or sum overflowed | `prev_spent + amount` or `action_count + 1` out of range |

## Mapping to the SDK

The SDK surfaces these as thrown errors during `pay()` / `submit`, and as a
`blocked` `StatusEvent` when an action can't be proven at all — for example, a
recipient outside the allowlist, which fails locally before any of the codes above
can be returned.
