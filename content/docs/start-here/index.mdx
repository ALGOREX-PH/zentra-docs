Autonomous agents are starting to move real money. The moment they do, one
question matters more than any other:

> Did this specific action, right now, actually obey the rules — without exposing
> the private details?

**Zentra** answers it with a proof. Before an agent can trigger a Stellar
payment, it must generate a zero-knowledge proof that the action satisfies a
**private**, user-defined policy — approved recipients, a per-invoice cap, a
daily spend limit, an invoice match, and a single-use nullifier — and that proof
is **bound to the agent's authoritative on-chain state**, so it cannot lie about
what it has already spent. A Soroban contract verifies the proof, checks the
state binding, enforces nullifier uniqueness, settles the payment, and emits a
verifiable receipt.

<Callout title="The promise">
Let agents act. Make them prove it. — **No proof, no payment.**
</Callout>

## Every action a proof

Identity tells you *who* an agent is. Permissions tell you *what* it may do.
Neither tells you whether a specific payment, happening right now, actually
followed the rules — or whether a prompt-injected agent is about to drain a
treasury while looking perfectly authorized.

Zentra closes that gap by making compliance something an agent must **prove**,
not something you reconstruct from logs afterward:

> Every action a proof. Every proof a receipt. Every receipt reputation.
