title: What Zentra is not
description: The boundary of what the protocol claims — stated plainly.
---

Trust comes as much from what a system refuses to claim as from what it promises.
Zentra has a deliberately narrow job.

<Callout title="The boundary statement">
Zentra is a **proof-of-compliance and settlement layer**. It is **not** an
identity system, an oracle, a policy author, a key manager, or a full compliance
engine.
</Callout>

## What that means in practice

- **Not an identity system.** Zentra verifies that an action obeyed a policy; it
  does not establish *who* the agent is. Bring your own identity — the agent's
  Stellar account authorizes the call via `require_auth`.
- **Not an oracle.** It proves statements about values you supply (amount,
  recipient, invoice); it does not fetch or attest to off-chain facts.
- **Not a policy author.** You define the policy and its thresholds. Zentra makes
  the policy *enforceable in zero knowledge*; it does not decide what is sensible.
- **Not a key manager.** Signing keys and their custody are yours. Zentra never
  sees or holds them.
- **Not a full compliance engine.** It enforces the specific, provable rules a
  policy encodes — not open-ended regulatory judgment.

## What it *is*

A precise primitive: given a private policy and an agent's authoritative on-chain
state, Zentra lets the agent prove — and the chain verify — that a proposed
payment is compliant, then settles it and emits a receipt. Narrow on purpose, so
it can be trusted completely within that scope.
