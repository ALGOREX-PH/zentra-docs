---
title: State binding
description: How a proof is tied to the agent's real on-chain state, making over-spend impossible.
---

State binding is what separates Zentra from a naive "does this payment fit the
limit?" check. The proof carries the agent's claimed prior state, and the
contract refuses to trust it unless it matches reality.

## The three bound signals

Every proof includes:

- `prevEpochId` — the epoch the prior counters belong to
- `prevSpent` — spend before this action
- `prevActionCount` — action count before this action

Inside `authorize_action`, the contract computes the **effective prior state**
from its own stored `AuthorityState` (applying epoch rollover), then asserts:

```text
prevEpochId      == effective.epoch_id
prevSpent        == effective.spent_in_epoch
prevActionCount  == effective.action_count
```

Any mismatch returns `StateMismatch`, and the payment is rejected.

## Why this stops over-spend

A compromised agent might try to slip a payment under the daily limit by claiming
it has spent nothing yet. But `prevSpent` in the proof must equal the contract's
real figure. Claim `prevSpent = 0` when the chain says `400`, and the assertion
fails — no money moves.

<Callout type="warn" title="This is Panel C">
Spend 400 of a 500 limit, then try to pay 150 while claiming `prevSpent = 0`. The
proof is internally valid, but the state binding rejects it on-chain. Lying about
history is not possible.
</Callout>
