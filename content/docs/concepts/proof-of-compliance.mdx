---
title: Proof of compliance
description: A zero-knowledge proof that a proposed action obeyed the private policy and the current on-chain state.
---

A **proof of compliance** is a zero-knowledge proof that a *proposed* payment
satisfies two things at once:

1. the agent's **private policy** — approved recipients, per-invoice cap, daily
   limit, and an invoice match; and
2. the agent's **current Authority State** on-chain — how much it has already
   spent this epoch, and how many actions it has taken.

The proof reveals neither of them. A verifier learns only that the action is valid.

## Proposed, proven, settled

Traditional automation acts and then logs. Zentra inserts a proof between intent
and settlement:

- **Proposed** — the agent assembles an action: recipient, amount, invoice.
- **Proven** — it generates a Groth16 proof that the action obeys the policy and
  matches the on-chain state.
- **Settled** — the contract verifies the proof, checks the state binding, and
  only then moves the money and emits a receipt.

<Callout>
Because the proof is bound to on-chain state, "I'm under my daily limit" is
checked against the real ledger — not a number the agent supplied.
</Callout>

## Why zero-knowledge

A policy is a sensitive asset: your vendor list, your limits, your invoice
details. Zentra proves the action obeyed those rules without publishing any of
them. Verification is public; the policy stays private.
