---
title: Privacy model
description: Exactly what Zentra keeps private and what it makes public.
---

Zentra's value is that verification is **public** while the policy is **private**.
Here is the precise split.

| Stays private | Becomes public |
| --- | --- |
| The full approved-recipient list | The recipient Merkle `recipientRoot` |
| Policy thresholds (`maxAmount`, `dailyLimit`) | The policy **commitment** |
| The secret policy `salt` | — |
| Daily-spend history / running totals | The post-action counters in the receipt |
| Invoice contents (the preimage) | The invoice **hash** |
| The agent's instruction context | That the action was **valid** |

## What a verifier learns

A verifier — the contract, or anyone reading the chain — learns only that:

- a valid proof exists for a registered policy commitment,
- the action matched the agent's authoritative on-chain state,
- the nullifier was unused, and
- the payment was therefore eligible to settle.

It learns **nothing** about the thresholds, the vendor list, or the agent's
reasoning.

<Callout>
The settled payment itself (amount, recipient) is visible on Stellar's public
ledger, as any payment is. What stays hidden is the *policy* that authorized it.
</Callout>
