title: Policy & commitment
description: A private policy, reduced to a single on-chain Poseidon commitment.
---

A **policy** is the rulebook for an agent's payments. It is authored privately and
never published in full.

## What a policy contains

- `name` — a human label.
- `asset` — what may be spent (the demo uses native XLM).
- `maxAmount` — the cap per invoice.
- `dailyLimit` — the cap per epoch.
- `approvedRecipients` — the allowlist, up to 16 addresses.
- `epochSeconds` — how long an epoch lasts (default one day).
- `salt` — a **secret**, high-entropy value that hides the policy.

## The commitment

Registering a policy publishes only a **commitment** — one field element —
together with the recipient Merkle root:

```text
policyCommitment = Poseidon(maxAmount, dailyLimit, recipientRoot, assetField, salt)
```

The thresholds and the salt stay private. Because the salt is secret, the
commitment is *hiding* — nobody can reconstruct the policy from it — and *binding*
— the agent can't later prove against a different policy.

<Callout title="Why a salt">
Without it, anyone could brute-force small limit values and recover your policy
from its commitment. The salt makes the commitment reveal nothing.
</Callout>

Every proof **opens** this commitment in zero knowledge: it shows the agent knows
a policy whose commitment was registered, without revealing the policy itself.
